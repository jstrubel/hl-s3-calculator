Important: This is a speculative model. Actual S3 mechanics are unknown and may differ significantly from these estimates. This is not financial advice.

Team Unlock Consideration

Team token vesting began November 2025 (~10M HYPE/month). This creates baseline sell pressure that may influence S3 timing and size. A larger S3 at lower prices vs. smaller S3 at higher prices achieves similar USD distribution while managing market impact.

Vesting Structure Scenarios

Unlike Genesis (which was 100% instant), S3 may include vesting to manage sell pressure. This model explores three scenarios:

Scenario Instant Vested Probability Rationale
No Vesting 100% 0% ~40% Matches Genesis precedent, simpler, "fair launch" ethos
50/50 Split 50% 50% ~35% Balances liquidity needs with sell pressure management
25/75 Split 25% 75% ~20% Aggressive if larger S3 size (7-8%), aligns with team vesting

Key Considerations:

  • Sell Pressure: At 6% S3 with 40% sell rate, ~$720M pressure at $30. Vesting spreads this over 12 months.
  • Tax Implications: In many jurisdictions, vested tokens are taxable at grant price - this may affect user preference.
  • Portfolio Margin Synergy: Locked HYPE could serve as collateral in the upcoming portfolio margin system.
  • Optional Lock Bonus: Team may offer higher allocation for voluntary lock (e.g., 1.3x for 6mo, 1.5x for 12mo lock).

Why Spot Gets 15% (vs ~6% of Revenue)

While spot trading generates only ~6% of platform revenue, there are strategic reasons to allocate ~2.5x its revenue share:

  • Collateral integrity: BLP and unified margin use Unit assets - thin spot books create liquidation cascade risk
  • Marginal utility: Additional spot liquidity has higher marginal value than additional perps liquidity (perps already deep)
  • Capital opportunity cost: Spot MMs lock real capital without leverage, requiring compensation vs. capital-efficient perps
  • Ecosystem balance: Hyperliquid wants to grow into a full L1, not just a perps DEX - spot is underdeveloped

  • Required for efficient liquidations during volatility

HyperEVM Allocation Uncertainty

The EVM allocation methodology is the most uncertain aspect of this model. Key questions:

  • Metric: Is it TVL-based, transaction-based, gas-spent, or protocol-specific?
  • S2 treatment: Was S2 supposed to be the "EVM season"? If so, has it already been distributed?
  • Double-dipping: Can EVM TVL that's also generating spot/perps volume count twice?

We model 8-10% allocation to EVM as a bootstrap incentive, but confidence is low. EVM users should apply wider confidence intervals to their estimates.
